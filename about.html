About

In January 2018, Theo Walcott transfered to Everton for over £20 million. That's roughly one and three seventeenths of a Ronaldo, you know the good one, that liked hamburgers and supermodels and didn't need to rip his shirt off everytime he toe-poked a goal home because he wasn't insecure.

Football - it has to be said - has become slightly absurd, very much outrageous and has more than a whiff of downright obscenity. How can one human being garner a value of nearly £200 million pounds whilst we still can't seem to cure cancer, or hangovers, or the Glaswegian accent.

Yes, Neymar, football's equivalent of a mating peacock, is worth twelve and a bit real Ronaldos. Twelve and a bit real Ronaldos! Sorry, but how is Neymar worth twelve and a bit Ronaldos! I mean maybe three and seven ninths of a Neymar might buy you a Ronaldo. However, twelve and a bit real Ronaldos! Nah.

Not that I'm here to knock Neymar, I mean he is an excellent footballer - albeit a repulsive human being, but hey ho, in the twenty-first century we can't have everything. I'm not knocking a Portugese sex-God, a Catalonian smurf, or Gigi Buffon, whose been around long enough to make it into this particular hall of fame.

What we're here for is to hear your stories about the real heroes of football, I'm pretty sure that the Palace fan that got kung-fu kicked by Cantona forgave him, because, well... It's Cantona, a flawed genius with a mad temper.

We want to talk about Pirlo's beard, Jurgen Klopp's 100-yard stare, Rino Gattusso's clear love of unicorns, how Nesta and Cannavaro need a genetically modified love-child and can you find Edgar David's goggles on eBay?
